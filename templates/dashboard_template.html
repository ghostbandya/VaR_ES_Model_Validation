Methodology

VaR (99%, 1-day): Basel backtesting standard.
Expected Shortfall (97.5%): FRTB Internal Models Approach standard.
Estimation window: trailing 250 trading days, rolled forward daily (fully out-of-sample).

Historical Simulation — empirical quantile of trailing 250-day realized portfolio P&L, no distributional assumption.
Parametric (Variance-Covariance) — assumes joint-normal asset returns; portfolio variance from the trailing covariance matrix, VaR/ES from closed-form Normal quantiles.
Monte Carlo — 5,000 correlated draws per day from a multivariate Student-t distribution, with degrees of freedom re-fit each window from the trailing realized kurtosis (captures fat tails that Parametric misses).

Kupiec POF & Christoffersen Tests — Full Sample

Kupiec tests whether the exception rate matches the model's stated confidence level (unconditional coverage). Christoffersen tests whether exceptions are independent over time rather than clustering (a model can pass Kupiec yet still fail independence if breaches bunch up in stress periods). Conditional coverage combines both. All tests at 95% significance.

Basel Traffic-Light Framework

Regulatory zone based on the count of 99% VaR exceptions in the trailing 250 trading days: Green 0–4 (model consistent with 99% confidence), Yellow 5–9 (possible capital multiplier add-on), Red 10+ (model presumed inaccurate, requires remediation).

Champion vs. Challenger Divergence

Champion model: Historical Simulation (assumption-free, Model Risk's typical default). Parametric and Monte Carlo are run as challengers; days where a challenger's VaR differs from the champion's by more than 20% are flagged for review.

Large, persistent divergence — especially Parametric consistently sitting below the champion — is itself a validation finding: it suggests the Normal-distribution assumption understates tail risk relative to what actually happened in the data.

Rolling Breach Rate (Drift Monitoring)

Trailing 250-day exception rate per model vs. the expected 1% rate. Sustained drift above the dashed line signals the model is falling out of calibration and warrants re-estimation or escalation.

Risk Calibration Ratio

Ratio of trailing 60-day realized P&L volatility to each model's VaR-implied volatility. A ratio above 1 means realized risk is running hotter than the model predicts (potential VaR breach clustering ahead); below 1 means the model is conservative.
